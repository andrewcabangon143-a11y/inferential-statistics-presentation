Population

Meaning: The population is the entire group you want to study.

Example: All 500 students in a school are the population.

Sampling Techniques

Sampling is the process of selecting a smaller group (the sample) from a much larger group (the population) to represent the whole.

1. Probability Sampling

Best for: Reducing bias and making general claims about a population.

  • Simple Random Sampling: Every individual has an equal chance of being picked (e.g., picking names from a hat).
  • Systematic Sampling: Selecting every nth individual from a list (e.g., every 10th person).
  • Stratified Sampling: Dividing the population into groups (strata) and picking a random sample from each group to ensure balance.
  • Cluster Sampling: Dividing the population into groups (clusters), randomly picking a few clusters, and studying everyone inside them.

2. Non-Probability Sampling

Best for: Exploratory research, pilot studies, or when time/budget is limited.

  • Convenience Sampling: Using participants who are easiest to reach or find.
  • Purposive (Judgmental) Sampling: Selecting specific people because they have the exact knowledge or traits you need.
  • Snowball Sampling: Asking current participants to refer people they know to the study (common for hard-to-reach groups).
  • Quota Sampling: Setting a target number for different types of people (e.g., 50 men, 50 women) but picking them non-randomly.
  • Voluntary Response Sampling: Letting people choose to join the study themselves (e.g., an online survey link).

Key Takeaways

  • Population vs Sample: The population is the whole group; the sample is the portion you actually measure to make inferences.
  • Probability Sampling: Use this when you want to minimize bias and generalize findings to the whole population.
  • Non-Probability Sampling: Use this for quick research where statistical generalization is not the main goal.
  • Slovin‚Äôs Usage: Best for simple, student-level research where only population size is known.
  • Raosoft Usage: Essential for academic surveys where confidence levels must be reported.

Final Rule of Thumb

If you need a quick number for a small project, use Slovin. If you are conducting a formal survey that needs to be defended in a thesis, use Raosoft.
